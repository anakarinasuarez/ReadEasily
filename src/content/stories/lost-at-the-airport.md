title: Lost at the Airport

I had never traveled abroad alone before, so my heart was racing as I walked into the huge airport.

People were rushing in every direction, pulling suitcases and shouting in languages I did not understand.

I had a connecting flight to catch, and I only had forty minutes to find the right gate.

I looked up at the giant screen that listed all the departures, but the words seemed to blur together.

My gate was B22, or at least I thought it was. I started walking quickly, following the bright signs above me.

After ten minutes, I realized that I had been going in the wrong direction the whole time.

I stopped in the middle of the busy hall, feeling completely lost. My phone had died, so I could not check the map.

A wave of panic rose in my chest. If I missed this flight, I would be stuck in this city overnight.

Then I noticed a woman in a blue uniform who was helping another traveler nearby. I hurried toward her.

"Excuse me," I said, trying to stay calm. "I am looking for gate B22, and my flight leaves very soon."

She smiled kindly and looked at my boarding pass. "You are in the wrong terminal," she explained gently.

My heart sank, but she quickly told me to take the train that connected the two buildings.

"You still have time," she said, "if you run." So I thanked her and ran as fast as I could.

The train arrived within a minute, and I jumped on, holding my bag tightly against my chest.

When I finally reached gate B22, the staff were making the last call for my flight.

I handed over my pass, completely out of breath, and stepped onto the plane just in time.

As I sank into my seat, I promised myself that I would always charge my phone before traveling.

I had learned that a kind stranger can turn a disaster into nothing more than a good story.
