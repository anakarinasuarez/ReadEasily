Last spring, my sister Clara and I decided to take a trip to the mountains.

We had been planning the journey for months, and finally the day arrived. We packed our bags the night before, barely able to sleep.

Early in the morning, while the sky was still gray, we drove out of the city. The roads were empty, and a soft mist hung over the fields.

As we climbed higher, the air grew cooler and cleaner. Tall pine trees stood on both sides of the winding road.

"Look at that," Clara said, pointing through the window. A river was running beside us, sparkling in the early light.

We reached a small village that sat quietly at the foot of the mountains. The people there were warm and welcoming.

An old woman who owned a little guesthouse offered us hot tea and fresh bread. We ate happily after the long drive.

The next morning, we started our hike. The path was steep, and we had to stop often to catch our breath.

I had never walked so far in my life, but the views kept me going. Every turn revealed something more beautiful.

By the afternoon, we reached the top of the hill. The whole valley spread out below us like a green carpet.

We sat on a flat rock and shared a simple lunch of cheese and apples. I have rarely felt so peaceful.

"I am so glad we came," Clara whispered. I nodded, because words did not feel necessary in that moment.

On the way down, dark clouds began to gather above the peaks. We hurried back before the rain could catch us.

That evening, we sat by the fire and listened to the rain falling softly on the roof.

If you ever feel tired of the noisy city, I think the mountains will heal something inside you.

We came home with sore legs and full hearts, already dreaming of our next trip together.
