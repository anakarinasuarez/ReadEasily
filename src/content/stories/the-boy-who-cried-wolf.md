On a green hill near a small village, a young boy watched the sheep every day.

The work was quiet, and sometimes the boy felt bored. He wanted some excitement.

One afternoon, he had an idea. He ran down the hill and shouted, "Wolf! Wolf! A wolf is chasing the sheep!"

The villagers heard him and came running. They carried sticks to help the boy. But there was no wolf.

The boy laughed and laughed. "I tricked you!" he said. The villagers were not happy, and they walked back home.

The next day, the boy was bored again. So he shouted the same words. "Wolf! Wolf! Please help me!"

Again the villagers ran up the hill. Again there was no wolf. The boy laughed, but the people were angry now.

"Do not lie to us," they said. "It is not a game." Then they went back to the village.

A few days later, a real wolf came out of the forest. It was big and gray, and it moved toward the sheep.

The boy was very scared. He shouted as loud as he could. "Wolf! Wolf! Please come quickly!"

But this time, nobody came. The villagers heard him, but they thought he was lying again.

The boy could not save all the sheep. He sat down and cried.

That evening, an old farmer found him on the hill. "We did not come," the farmer said gently, "because you lied before."

When you always tell lies, people stop believing the truth.
