All summer long, the sun was warm and the fields were green.

A little ant worked hard every day. She carried food to her home. She collected seeds and grains because winter was coming.

A grasshopper sat in the grass and played his music. He was happy and lazy.

"Why do you work so hard?" he asked the ant. "Come and sing with me!"

"I am saving food for the winter," the ant said. "You should save food too, because the cold days are coming."

The grasshopper laughed. "Winter is far away. I am going to enjoy the sunshine now."

So the grasshopper played all day, and the ant kept working.

The summer ended, and the autumn leaves fell. Then the winter came. The wind was cold, and snow covered the fields.

The grasshopper had no food. He was hungry and cold. He could not find a single seed.

He remembered the busy little ant. He walked to her warm home and knocked on the door.

"Please help me," he said. "I have no food, and I am very cold."

The kind ant opened her door. She gave him a warm meal and a place by the fire.

"Thank you," the grasshopper said softly. "Next summer, I am going to work like you."

The ant smiled. "We can share today, but tomorrow we must both be ready."

Hard work in good times keeps us safe in hard times.
