It was almost eight o'clock, and Tom was already late for work.

He put on his coat and reached into his pocket for his keys. But the pocket was empty.

"Oh no," he said. "Where are my keys?" He looked on the table by the door. They were not there.

Tom checked the kitchen. He looked next to the coffee cup and under the newspaper. Still no keys.

He was getting worried, because his bus was leaving very soon. He took a deep breath and tried to think.

"Where did I go last night?" he asked himself. He remembered the sofa, so he looked under the cushions.

He found some coins and an old pen, but he did not find his keys.

Then Tom looked in his jacket from yesterday. The pockets were empty too. He sat down and felt tired.

His cat, Bella, was watching him quietly from the window. She jumped down and walked to her food bowl.

Next to the bowl, something shiny caught the light. It was his keys! They were on the floor in the corner.

Tom laughed with relief. "Bella, did you push my keys down here?" The cat only blinked at him.

He grabbed the keys and ran to the door. He caught his bus just in time.

That evening, Tom bought a small hook for the wall. Now his keys always have a home.
