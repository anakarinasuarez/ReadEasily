Mr. Park lived alone in a small house at the end of a quiet street.

One morning, his daughter brought him a gift. It was a small white robot with round blue eyes.

"This is Pip," she said. "He can help you around the house." Mr. Park was not sure about the idea.

"I do not need a robot," he said. But he smiled politely and said thank you.

The first day, Pip rolled around the kitchen. "Good morning," it said. "Would you like some tea?"

Mr. Park was surprised. "Yes, please," he answered. Soon the robot made a warm cup of tea.

Every day, Pip helped a little more. It turned on the lights and reminded him to take his medicine.

"Time for your pills, Mr. Park," it said kindly. The old man started to like his new friend.

In the evenings, Pip read stories aloud. Mr. Park listened and closed his eyes. The house felt less empty now.

One cold night, Mr. Park slipped near the stairs. He could not get up by himself.

"Do not worry," said Pip. "I am calling your daughter now." Help arrived very quickly.

After that day, Mr. Park trusted Pip completely. They watched the news and shared quiet mornings together.

"You are not just a machine," he told Pip one day. "You are good company."

Sometimes the right help comes from the most surprising places.
